Scientific methodology

The atlas uses a transparent public-source benchmarking method. Laboratories are included when there is public evidence that they support one or more CBRN-relevant functions: anti-CBRN defense, national control or verification, calibration and testing of CBRN items, reference diagnostics, high-containment research, radiation or nuclear safety, or applied R&D for preparedness and response.

Inclusion logic

Candidate labs are selected by mission relevance, institutional mandate, domain coverage, and public evidence. The dataset separates confirmed evidence from seed assumptions.

Scoring dimensions

Strategic relevance, technical capability, accreditation/standards, operational support, research output, international collaboration, and infrastructure maturity.

Evidence grading

Priority is given to official government/institutional sources, standards participation, public reports, peer-reviewed literature, and recognized reference-lab status.

Domain classification

Each lab is tagged against Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear capability. Multi-domain labs are treated separately from single-domain reference labs.

Mission typology

Defense, R&D, public health, calibration, verification, testing, biosurveillance, response support, and regulatory monitoring are distinguished.

Validation rule

A profile should not be treated as official until each score is linked to evidence and reviewed by a subject-matter expert.
