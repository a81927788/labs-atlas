Scientific methodology

Why these criteria and weights are used

Strategic relevance · 22%

Highest weight because this atlas is for CBRN defense decision support. A laboratory matters most when it supports national preparedness, defense planning, reference authority, threat assessment, or direct CBRN control missions.

Technical capability · 20%

Near-equal to strategy because credible benchmarking requires evidence of actual C/B/R/N domain depth, analytical competence, specialized measurement, and ability to generate defensible scientific outputs.

Operational support · 16%

Weighted strongly because laboratories add defense value when they can support incident response, field confirmation, warning and reporting, calibration, forensic analysis, or operational decision-making.

Accreditation standards · 12%

Medium weight because quality systems, traceability, biosafety, radiation safety, ISO-style governance, and reference-lab status improve trust and interoperability, but may be underreported publicly.

Research output · 12%

Research output shows sustained knowledge generation through publications, technical reports, methods, patents, datasets, or programs. It should matter, but not dominate mission relevance.

Infrastructure maturity · 10%

Facilities, containment, measurement systems, specialized equipment, and sample-handling maturity are essential enablers, but public sources may not expose the full infrastructure picture.

International collaboration · 8%

Collaboration improves interoperability, credibility, and access to networks, but it receives the lowest weight because some sensitive defense laboratories intentionally limit public collaboration visibility.
