Scientific methodology

The atlas benchmarks laboratories by their functional role in a CBRN defense ecosystem rather than by reputation alone. Inclusion and scoring are organized around three main objectives: anti/control/defense CBRN, checking and calibration of CBRN items, and research and development for CBRN defense.

Inclusion logic

A laboratory is listed when it has public or candidate evidence of relevance to CBRN defense, national control, reference analysis, calibration/testing, high-containment work, radiation/nuclear safety, or applied preparedness R&D.

Comparison basis

Labs are compared using mission relevance, C/B/R/N domain coverage, technical capability, infrastructure maturity, operational integration, research output, standards/accreditation evidence, and international collaboration.

Weighted scoring

V1 score uses: strategic relevance 20%, technical capability 20%, accreditation/standards 15%, operational support 15%, research 10%, collaboration 10%, infrastructure 10%.

Evidence hierarchy

Official government or institutional sources are strongest, followed by recognized reference-lab records, standards/accreditation listings, peer-reviewed papers, public reports, and vendor/government program descriptions.

Domain classification

Each profile is tagged Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear, or multi-domain. Domain tags should be refined only when evidence confirms the capability area.

Validation status

V1 separates structure from proof. A lab can be a strong benchmark candidate while still requiring official URL, accreditation, facility, program, or publication evidence before executive use.
